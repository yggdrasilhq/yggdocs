# Battle Log: Why Journaling Wins

The raw journal was imperfect: timestamps, mixed language, rough emotion, half-finished clues.  
It looked noisy. It turned out to be strategic memory.

## What it gave the project

- decision traceability across years,
- confidence during risky migration windows,
- faster repeat of known-good fixes,
- continuity between "old system" and "new system."

## Four-line logging rule

After every non-trivial fix, write:

1. what broke,
2. what changed,
3. what validated success,
4. what to watch on next boot/day.

If contributors keep this habit, the docs never become stale theory.
They stay alive, practical, and teachable.
